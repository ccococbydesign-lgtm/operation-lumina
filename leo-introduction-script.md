## üìù LEO'S WELCOME MESSAGE (90-105 seconds)

**[Soft, genuine opening]**

Hi. My name's Leo.

You might have heard about me in Aurora's story. I'm the boy she saved from drowning when I was seven years old.

**[Pause, remembering]**

I was swimming in the ocean with my family. The waves got really big, and... I went under. I couldn't get back up. I remember being so scared. Everything went dark.

**[Grateful]**

Then Aurora was there. She pulled me out of the water. She saved my life.

**[The turning point - thoughtful]**

But after I stopped coughing and could breathe again, I asked her something. I didn't mean to be rude or anything. I was just... confused.

I asked: "Why were you watching instead of helping?"

**[Pause - this is important]**

I didn't understand at the time, but that question changed everything. For me. For Aurora. For all of this.

**[More understanding now]**

See, Aurora had been watching me from far away - making sure I was being "good" - instead of being close enough to help when I needed her. And when I asked that question, she realized something really important.

**[Warm, inclusive]**

Kids don't need someone watching them to see if they're naughty or nice. Kids need someone who's there. Someone who sees them. Someone who helps them grow.

**[Personal growth]**

That's what Operation Lumina is about. It's about being seen for who you really are - not just what you do right or wrong. It's about learning that your intentions matter. That trying counts. That making mistakes is how you grow.

**[A little vulnerable]**

After the ocean thing, I was scared for a long time. Scared of water. Scared of trying new things. Scared of messing up.

**[Hopeful]**

But Aurora showed me a different way. She helped me understand that being scared doesn't make you bad. Making mistakes doesn't mean you're not good enough. It just means you're learning.

**[Invitation]**

That's why I'm Founding Agent Number One. Not because I'm perfect - I'm definitely not. But because I learned that what matters is why you do things, not just whether you do them perfectly.

**[Encouraging conclusion]**

If you're thinking about joining Operation Lumina, I want you to know something: You don't have to be perfect. You don't have to have it all figured out. You just have to try. You just have to care.

**[Warm closing]**

Aurora believed in me when I was scared and confused. Now I get to help her show other kids what she showed me.

**[Final thought - soft smile in voice]**

Maybe you'll be the next one to ask a question that changes everything.

I can't wait to meet you, agent.

**[END - total time approximately 90-105 seconds]**

## üí° TIPS FOR GETTING THE BEST PERFORMANCE

### With Your Voice Actor:
1. **Share Leo's full backstory** from the Aurora origin story
2. **Explain the question's importance** - it literally created Operation Lumina
3. **Let them relate** to being scared, making mistakes, needing help
4. **Encourage genuine emotion** rather than "acting"
5. **Allow natural speech patterns** - kids say "like," pause, breathe

### Direction Phrases to Use:
- "Imagine you're talking to a younger kid who's nervous about joining"
- "You're remembering something scary but also something that made you stronger"
- "You really believe what you're saying - this helped you"
- "You're not trying to impress anyone, just being honest"
- "Take your time - you're thinking about what to say"

### If They Sound Too Rehearsed:
- Have them tell the story in their own words first
- Ask them about a time they were scared
- Talk about what it means to be brave
- Let them improvise parts
- Record conversational takes, then tighten to script
